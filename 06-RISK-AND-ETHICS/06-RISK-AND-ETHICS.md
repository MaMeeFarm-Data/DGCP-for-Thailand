# DGCP™ Risk & Ethics Framework for Thailand  
**Ensuring Safety, Integrity, Rights, and Transparent Data Governance**

---

## 1. Introduction

This document defines the **Risk & Ethics Framework** for DGCP™  
(Data Governance & Continuous Proof) in Thailand.

Its purpose is to ensure that:

- DGCP™ protects citizens  
- prevents government or platform abuse  
- supports ethical AI  
- minimizes systemic risks  
- provides national trust and accountability  

The framework aligns with global principles from OECD, UNESCO,  
and international data rights standards.

---

## 2. Ethical Foundations

DGCP™ follows four ethical pillars:

### 2.1 Human Data Sovereignty  
Citizens own their Real-World Data (RWD) at all times.

### 2.2 Transparency and Accountability  
Every action in the system is timestamped and auditable.

### 2.3 Community Governance  
Local validators share responsibility for oversight.

### 2.4 Non-Discrimination  
DGCP™ ensures equal access and avoids algorithmic bias.

---

## 3. Major Risks and Mitigations

This section outlines the primary risks to DGCP™  
and how the architecture prevents them.

---

## 3.1 Risk: Government Overreach  
**Threat:**  
A government entity might attempt to access or control citizen data.

**Mitigation:**  
- Immutable timestamps prevent data manipulation  
- Hash/CID verification exposes alterations  
- Licensing prohibits unauthorized access  
- Community validators prevent central control  
- Audit logs required for every government query  

DGCP™ is structurally resistant to political abuse.

---

## 3.2 Risk: Platform Exploitation  
**Threat:**  
Foreign or domestic platforms may extract RWD without compensation.

**Mitigation:**  
- MMFARM-POL-2025 enforces strict licensing terms  
- Provenance tags trace every dataset  
- Economic rights must be recognized before use  
- Violations are detectable and legally actionable  

RWD extraction without consent becomes impossible to hide.

---

## 3.3 Risk: Synthetic or Fake Data  
**Threat:**  
Actors may try to submit false or AI-generated data.

**Mitigation:**  
- Narrative validation  
- Metadata consistency checks  
- Community verification  
- Cross-proof continuity rules  
- Sensor/environment cross-checking  

DGCP™ favors human-generated evidence.

---

## 3.4 Risk: Identity Misuse  
**Threat:**  
Impersonation or false attribution of work.

**Mitigation:**  
- Multi-layer identity model  
- Optional pseudonymization  
- Worker-controlled keys  
- Validator attestation  

Identity and ownership remain secure.

---

## 3.5 Risk: AI Misuse  
**Threat:**  
AI companies or institutions may use DGCP™ data without attribution.

**Mitigation:**  
- License enforcement  
- Mandatory provenance markers  
- Legal penalties via copyright law  
- Hash-based auditing  
- Model training traceability  

AI must disclose DGCP™-origin data or risk violation.

---

## 4. Ethical Guidelines for Deployment

### 4.1 Consent & Permissions  
Citizens must control who accesses their RWD.

### 4.2 Minimization  
Only necessary metadata is collected.

### 4.3 Local Autonomy  
Communities define use-case boundaries.

### 4.4 Fair Access  
Government cannot prioritize certain groups unfairly.

### 4.5 Transparency Reporting  
Institutions must publish:

- what RWD they used  
- why they used it  
- how outcomes were affected

---

## 5. Community-Centric Safeguards

DGCP™ incorporates community governance:

### 5.1 Distributed Validators  
No single authority can rewrite history.

### 5.2 Public Visibility  
All changes/events can be logged transparently.

### 5.3 Traceability  
Every dataset carries:

- timestamp  
- hash  
- license  
- origin marker  
- narrative context  
- validator footprint  

This eliminates silent misuse.
